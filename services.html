What We Offer

Electrical Services

Residential and commercial electrical work done right, the first time. From a tripped breaker to a complete new-construction wiring job — Venora Electric has handled it all since 1960.

Service Categories

Residential

Wiring & Repairs

Complete home wiring for new construction and rewiring of older homes. We diagnose and fix any electrical fault quickly and safely.

Panel Upgrades

Replace aging fuse boxes and underpowered panels with modern circuit breaker panels. We handle 100A to 400A service upgrades to code.

Safety Inspections

Thorough inspection of your home’s electrical system, identifying hazards, code violations, and wear before they become dangerous.

Lighting Installation

Interior and exterior lighting design and installation, including recessed, landscape, and decorative fixtures with energy-efficient options.

EV Charger Installation

Level 2 home EV charger installation with dedicated 240V circuit. We handle permitting and inspection for a code-compliant setup.

Smoke & CO Detectors

Installation of interconnected hard-wired smoke and carbon monoxide detectors on every floor, meeting current CT fire code requirements.

Generator Hook-Up

Standby and portable generator connections with transfer switch installation. Keep your home powered when the grid goes down.

Commercial

Service Upgrades

Increase your building’s electrical capacity to support equipment, expansion, or modernization. From 200A to 800A service and beyond.

New Construction Wiring

Complete electrical rough-in and finish work for commercial new construction. We coordinate with your general contractor from ground up.

Maintenance Contracts

Scheduled preventive maintenance programs for commercial and multi-unit properties. Reduce downtime and catch problems before they escalate.

Lighting Retrofits

Upgrade fluorescent and HID lighting to energy-efficient LED throughout your facility. Reduce energy costs and maintenance demands.

Code Compliance Work

Bring your facility into compliance with current NEC and Connecticut electrical codes. We document all work for inspection and permitting.

Free Self-Assessment

Is Your Home’s Electrical System Safe?

Answer 10 quick yes/no questions to assess your home’s electrical risk level. Most homeowners are surprised by what they find.

Home Electrical Safety Checklist

Answer honestly. “Yes” answers indicate potential risk factors.

0 of 10 answered
1 My home was built before 1980.
2 I have a fuse box, not a circuit breaker panel.
3 I use extension cords as permanent wiring in some rooms.
4 Outlets near my sinks or bathrooms do not have GFCI protection.
5 My circuits trip or blow fuses more than once a month.
6 I have two-prong (ungrounded) outlets in my home.
7 My lights flicker or dim regularly.
8 I’ve noticed outlets or switches that feel warm to the touch.
9 I don’t have interconnected smoke detectors on every floor.
10 My home has never had a professional electrical inspection.
